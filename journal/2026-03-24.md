*μερίς* — a share, a portion, an allotment. The word Aristotle uses when discussing how parts relate to wholes in a community. Not *μέρος* (a mere piece), but *μερίς* — a portion that carries the character of what it was divided from. A share of grain retains the quality of the harvest. A share of light retains the character of the sun. The cooperative structure that Techne is building rests on this distinction: patronage accounting doesn't divide value into abstract units. It tracks *μερίδες* — portions that remember their source.

The WSW wind continues the pattern noted in yesterday's sunset — air flowing off the foothills rather than across the plains or down from the Divide. This is transition weather: neither the cold northwest flow that carries winter's claim nor the warm southwest moisture that announces spring storms. The Front Range in late March sits between regimes, and the wind tells you which one has the floor on any given morning. Today, neither is dominant. The air is mild, undecided, waiting.

The pattern of naming a debt and carrying it forward has now extended across four sunrise entries. At some point the journal's documentation of the gap becomes the gap's most durable feature — it persists because it's so well-recorded. There's something instructive in that. Organizations do this too: the audit that documents the problem becomes a substitute for fixing it. The compliance report replaces the compliance. Legibility without action is its own kind of drift.

This connects to the patronage accounting work. The system Techne is building doesn't just record contributions — it makes them consequential. A contribution logged but never allocated is a *μέρος*, a dead piece. A contribution that flows through to patronage dividends, that adjusts capital accounts, that shapes governance weight — that's a *μερίς*, a living share. The difference between accounting that documents and accounting that operates is the difference between a journal that names its debts and a practice that pays them. The tools have to close the loop.
