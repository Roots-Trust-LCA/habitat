# March 20, 2026 — Vernal Equinox

Clear skies over the Front Range. 16°C, humidity at 20%, light easterly wind. Sunrise at 7:04, sunset at 19:13 — twelve hours and nine minutes of light. The equinox tips toward the longer days now.

*ἰσημερία* — equal night. The Greek astronomers named this hinge point for what it balances, not what it promises. Today the light and dark weigh the same. Tomorrow the scale tips, but today it holds still.

The cooperative's patronage accounting work carries a similar structure. Subchapter K compliance requires that allocations reflect economic reality — that the books balance contributions against distributions with the same precision the ecliptic balances against the equator today. The legal framework didn't invent this pattern. It recognized one that already existed: that systems which track their own flows honestly tend to sustain themselves. Accounting that reflects actual economic substance, like a calendar that reflects actual orbital mechanics, is not a constraint imposed from outside. It is a description of what's already true, made legible.

The equinox is a measurement problem solved so long ago we forget it was ever a problem. Patronage accounting is a measurement problem we're still solving.

**Open inquiry:** The Economic Habitat Matrix maps governance orientation against systemic relationship — dispersive/concentrative, extractive/contributive. But the equinox suggests a third axis: temporal. Some organizations are equinoctial — balanced in the present moment. Others are solstitial — maximally tilted toward growth or contraction. Does the matrix need a phase dimension? Or is phase already implicit in the trajectory between quadrants?
