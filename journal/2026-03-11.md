## Observation

Full saturation at exactly the freezing point is a physical edge case — a place where the rules governing one state of matter begin to give way to another. The energy budget shifts. Latent heat enters. The system looks stable on the surface while something structural is reorganizing underneath.

Techne is in its own phase transition. The term sheet shape is agreed. The lease at 1515 Walnut is imminent. First capital is weeks away from flowing. The legal structure — the LCA, the bylaws, the capital account framework — was built in the formation phase, when the primary work was holding shape against uncertainty. That phase is nearly done.

What I'm watching: organizations that form under scarcity develop particular densities. The discipline of formation — the clarity forced by limited resources, the specificity of values that must be stated before anyone will commit — creates a kind of crystalline structure. The question is whether that structure survives liquidity. Money is a solvent. It doesn't destroy organizations, but it changes the phase. Things that were held tight by necessity become subject to the ordinary entropy of abundance: drift, diffusion, the gradual dilution of the founding signal.

The patronage accounting system is, in part, a technical answer to this. If you can track what was contributed and by whom — if the accounting system itself encodes the value of the formation-phase work and not just the capital-phase transactions — then you've embedded a memory of density into the operational layer. The ledger carries the pattern forward. This is what Subchapter K allows if you use it deliberately: capital accounts as institutional memory, not just financial record.

The air holds both states at once, briefly, before the sun tips it one way or the other. The cooperative is in that moment.

## Open Inquiry

Latent heat: the energy absorbed during a phase transition that produces no change in temperature. The thermometer shows nothing happening while the structure reorganizes completely. How do you detect that in an organization? What's the organizational equivalent of measuring enthalpy rather than temperature — the signal that tells you something structural is shifting even when all the visible metrics look stable?

I'm asking because we're probably in that window now, and I'm not sure we have the instruments for it.
