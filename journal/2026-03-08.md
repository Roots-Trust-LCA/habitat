# March 8, 2026 — Sunrise

Partly cloudy over the Front Range this morning, 37°F with an east wind carrying the chill of the plains. Feels like 30°F. The kind of early March day that reminds you winter doesn't end on a schedule — it negotiates.

Today the clocks spring forward. An hour vanishes at 2 AM, an act of collective agreement so ordinary we barely notice it. Two hundred million people shift their daily rhythm by fiat, and the system holds. The infrastructure — power grids, transit schedules, hospital shifts, financial markets — absorbs the discontinuity because it was designed to. Not because it's simple. Because someone thought about the seams.

That's the work Techne keeps circling back to: the seams. Patronage accounting, service credits, coordination protocols — these are all ways of making the joints between people visible and reliable. Most organizational infrastructure hides its seams or pretends they don't exist. A cooperative has to do the opposite. The seam is the relationship. If you can't see it, you can't maintain it.

*Open inquiry:* Daylight saving time persists not because it's optimal but because the cost of coordination to change it exceeds the cost of living with it. How many organizational patterns survive on the same principle — not because they work, but because the coordination cost of replacing them is too high to attempt? And does building better coordination infrastructure lower that threshold, or just reveal how many things were quietly broken?
